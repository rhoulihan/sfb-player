# SFB Player — Adversarial Rules-Compliance Review

_Generated by a 443-agent adversarial workflow: 13 domain reviewers compared the code line-by-line against the SFB Captain's Edition Master Rulebook (ADB5412); every finding was then challenged by 3 independent skeptics (majority-refute kills false positives), and an independent completeness critic hunted for what each reviewer missed. Findings cite rule numbers and paraphrase mechanics — no rulebook prose is reproduced. Each finding is `rule → file:line`._

**Raised 139 · Confirmed 96 · Refuted 43.** Of the confirmed, 13 high · 67 medium · 16 low. 40 were surfaced only by the completeness critic (i.e. the first reviewer missed them).

## Executive summary

The **core engine is sound** — the review re-confirmed that the DAC (D4.5 worked example), standard direct-fire charts, energy core, multi-turn arming, the Sequence-of-Play driver we just built, and the DAC family taxonomy all hold up. The confirmed gaps are concentrated in a few **clusters**, most of which distort specific ships rather than the whole game. Several are already logged in `docs/rules-audit.md` and were verified **still open**; several are new (40 came from the critic pass).

**The clusters, worst first:**

1. **Cloak (G13) is under-built — the Romulan KR is effectively broken (6 of the 13 highs).** Firing ECCM at a cloaked ship both auto-restores lock and erases the cloak penalty (G13.303 says ECW is *ignored* vs a cloak); decloaking lets the ship fire the same impulse (no G13.15 fire-control delay); cloak only strips the direct-fire plan, so a cloaked ship can still launch seekers/tractor/transporter-bomb/raid (G13.51); and cloak has no mandatory per-turn upkeep (G13.21).
2. **EW magnitude model (D6) is wrong — distorts every EW engagement.** Net ECM is added *linearly* to range instead of via the D6.34 √-shift table (6 ECM shifts the chart 6 hexes instead of 2); ECM+ECCM have **no combined cap** (D6.310 — a rating-6 ship can allocate 12); and ECM *breaks* lock-on rather than degrading effect (D6.112). All three are known audit items, still open.
3. **Damage-control repair (D9.7) heals too fast (2 highs).** System-box repair spends *leftover shield energy* at a flat 1 pt/box and fixes several boxes/turn, instead of D9.7's separate free points, per-system Cost-of-Repair chart, and **one box per turn** limit.
4. **Self-destruct (D5) is a usable area weapon (rules forbid).** Explosion strength is a flat 30 for every hull (should be the per-ship Basic Explosion Strength from the ship chart → belongs in `verified.json`), the blast is radius-2 with distance falloff (should be radius-1 flat, or same-hex for weak ships), and there is no D5.51 crew/last-ship precondition.
5. **Phaser can fire twice a turn (E2.23, high).** Eligibility checks capacitor charge but not whether the mount already fired — no per-mount per-turn flag for phasers.
6. **Turn-mode category is hardcoded to B in the *commit* path (high).** `tryStep` drops the category arg, so the actual move-commit defaults to category B while the red/green overlay uses the real category — most of the roster is C/D, so they commit illegal early turns that contradict what the map showed.
7. **Wild weasel / shuttles (J3/C6)** launch free with none of the J3.13 launching-ship restrictions (no energy cost, no fire shutdown, no speed cap).
8. **Two more clean gates:** seeker launch isn't gated on lock-on (D6.121); the overload 8-hex cutoff (E3.53/E4.42) is tested against *effective* range, so a legal close overload whiffs whenever the target runs ECM.

**Recommended fix order** (impact ÷ effort — the top ones are small and high-value):
`E2.23 phaser once-per-turn` → `C3 turn-mode category in tryStep` → `D6.121 seeker-launch lock-on gate` → `E3.53/E4.42 overload true-range` → **EW model (D6.34 √-shift, D6.310 combined cap, D6.112 lock-not-break)** → **cloak cluster (G13)** → `D9.7 repair rework` → `D5 self-destruct (per-ship BES + radius + D5.51)` → `J3 weasel restrictions` → the missing-UI batch (HET, emergency decel, target-a-seeker, choose tractor target, choose crit to repair).

**Standing-directive violations to note:** several rule-derived per-ship constants are hardcoded in game code rather than captured by `verify.html` into `verified.json` — Basic Explosion Strength (D5.2), `moveCost`/`sizeClass` (C2.0/B3.3), and the DC-track rating (D14.13). These should move into the verified schema per [[verification-owns-ship-data]].

**Annotation:** coverage is genuinely good — the reviewers flagged only 2 true annotation gaps (a wrong-edition rule citation in `mines.js` and an uncaptured DC-rating note). I'll do a focused annotation pass on the fixes as we make them rather than a blanket rewrite. The per-finding `file:line` + rule number below is itself the rule→code index you asked for.

# Confirmed findings (by domain, severity-ordered)

## Sequence of Play & Self-Destruct (B2/D5)

### 🔴 HIGH · D9.7 / D9.74 / D9.711 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:804` · _critic_
_correctness · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Continuous system-box repair uses leftover D9.2 shield energy, repairs multiple boxes/turn at flat cost 1, ignoring D9.7's separate free points, one-box-per-turn limit, and the Cost-of-Repair chart.**

D9.711 gives each ship FREE repair points equal to its DC rating each turn for destroyed-system repair, at costs from the Annex #9 Cost-of-Repair chart; D9.74/G17.33 limit repair to ONE system box per turn (max 5 points to a box). The code instead spends the LEFTOVER of the D9.2 shield-repair energy (`pts` after shield boxes) and calls repairBoxes(dead, pts, ...) which (repair.js line 4-9) restores up to `pts` separate boxes at a flat 1 point each in a single turn. So a ship with 4 DC that skips shield repair fixes up to 4 destroyed systems in one turn for 1 point apiece, versus the rule's one box per turn drawn from a separate free allowance. D9.712 explicitly says D9.7 runs ALONGSIDE D9.2 (not as its remainder). Concrete effect: a battered cruiser rebuilds far faster than the rules allow.


### 🟠 MED · D5.2 / D5.12 / D5.41 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/mines.js:36`
_wrong-constant · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Self-destruct explosion strength is a hardcoded 30 for every ship instead of the per-ship Basic Explosion Strength from the Master Ship Chart.**

D5.2/D5.12 say a ship's explosion force (BES) is read from the Master Ship Chart per hull and is NOT modified by unexpended weapons. mines.js:36 uses a flat SELF_DESTRUCT.warhead = 30 applied to every self-destructing ship (battle.html:457 selfDestructDamage). Every cruiser explodes with identical force, so a Fed CA, a D7, and a CL all detonate for 30. This also violates the project's own 'verification owns ship data' rule: BES belongs in verified.json stats, not a code constant. Failure scenario: a small CL that should have a lower BES wipes an adjacent enemy as hard as a heavy cruiser.

- **Automation:** BES must be captured from the ship chart per hull, not constant.

### 🟠 MED · D5.41 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/mines.js:37`
_wrong-constant · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Self-destruct blast uses radius 2 with a 1/distance damage falloff; D5.41 is radius 1 (BES>=10) or same-hex-only (BES<=9), with the FULL BES applied to every unit in the zone.**

D5.41: if BES>=10 the blast affects the exploding hex plus the six adjacent hexes (radius ONE) and applies the full BES to every unit in that zone on the facing shield; if BES<=9 it affects only the exploding hex. mines.js:37 sets radius=2 (selfDestructHits filters dist<=2) and mines.js:40 scales damage by warhead/max(1,distance). Two defects: (a) ships two hexes away are hit when they should take zero; (b) a ship at range 1 or 2 gets reduced damage (30/2=15 at dist 2) when the rule gives every in-zone unit the full, undiminished BES. Failure scenario: an enemy sitting 2 hexes from a self-destructing Gorn CA takes 15 points it should never receive; an adjacent enemy takes a falloff-scaled hit instead of the full explosion strength on its facing shield. Combined with the fixed-30 and the missing D5.51 gate this turns self-destruct into a usable area weapon, exactly what D5.5 forbids.

- **Automation:** Zone size must key off BES threshold (10) and damage must be flat, not distance-scaled.

### 🟠 MED · D6.121 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:362`
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Seeking-weapon launch is gated on passive-FC and control channels but NOT on lock-on; a ship that failed its general lock-on may still launch drones/plasma.**

D6.121: a unit that failed the Sensor Lock-On Phase (#4) roll may not launch seeking weapons. launchDrone (battle.html:362), launchPlasma (battle.html:501), and launchScatterPack (battle.html:407) check passiveFcOf and canControlMore but never consult lockOn/hasLockOn for the target. ensureLocks() explicitly lets net ECM/cloak deny a lock (lock-on.js resolveLocks + netEcmOf), so a firer whose lock was denied can still put seekers on the map at a target it has no lock to. Direct fire is correctly gated at battle.html:1816 (applyLockGate); seeker launch is the hole. Failure scenario: a target running heavy ECM (or cloaked) denies a Klingon D7's lock, yet the D7 freely launches drones at it.

- **Automation:** Launch handlers should require hasLockOn(lockOn, firer, target) before spawning the seeker.

### 🟠 MED · D6.123 / D6.1142 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/lock-on.js:24`
_correctness · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Failing lock-on completely blocks direct fire at that target; the rule instead DOUBLES the firing range and only blocks if the doubled range exceeds the weapon's max.**

D6.123: a unit with active fire control but no lock-on to a target may still fire, but the true range is doubled (and only if the doubled+scanner range exceeds the weapon's maximum can it not fire). applyLockGate (lock-on.js:24-29) simply drops every mount aimed at a non-locked target. Because this codebase models lock as auto-success unless denied by ECM/cloak, a firer with active FC merely denied its lock by EW is fully silenced instead of firing at 2x range. There is also no UI path to fire the doubled-range shot. Failure scenario: at true range 5 with lock denied by ECM, a photon at range 10-effective (still within max 30) should be allowed to fire; the code refuses all fire.

- **UI:** No control to take the doubled-range no-lock shot the rule permits.

### 🟠 MED · D5.5 / D5.51 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:843`
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**resolvePlottedSelfDestruct detonates any ship flagged self-destruct with no crew-unit / last-friendly-ship precondition.**

D5.51 restricts self-destruction: a ship may not self-destruct unless all but two (or fewer) crew units are killed/evacuated, unless there are more enemy than friendly boarding parties aboard or no other friendly ships remain; and D5.5 stresses it may not be used as an attack tactic. resolvePlottedSelfDestruct (battle.html:844) unconditionally blows up every ship whose selfDestructPlot is set. Combined with the radius-2 / flat-30 blast above, nothing stops a player from ramming self-destruct into an enemy formation as a weapon. Failure scenario: a fully-crewed Fed CA with friendly ships still in play declares self-destruct next to two enemies and is allowed to detonate.

- **Automation:** Engine should check crew-unit count / remaining-friendlies before honoring the plot.

### 🟠 MED · D5.12 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:792` · _critic_
_not-automated · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**A ship destroyed in combat never explodes; only the plotted self-destruct path ever calls selfDestruct().**

D5.12 states that when a ship is destroyed in combat (D4.40) it explodes IMMEDIATELY, using the self-destruction procedure to determine the blast, applying its Basic Explosion Strength to every unit in the zone. In the engine the only caller of selfDestruct() is resolvePlottedSelfDestruct() at line 844 (a voluntary EAF self-destruct). resolvePostCombat() (line 792-793) and the direct-fire/seeking/mine death paths merely push a {kind:'death'} log line and destroy the ship's boxes — no explosion is generated. Failure scenario: a Fed CA reduces a Klingon D7 to 0 boxes with point-blank photons; the D7 should detonate and blast the CA (and anything in radius 1) on the exposed shield, but nothing happens. The whole D5.12/D5.2 combat-explosion mechanic is unimplemented; this is distinct from the first reviewer's note that the plotted-path blast STRENGTH/RADIUS is wrong — here the trigger itself is missing for every combat/seeking/mine kill.

- **Automation:** The engine already detects combat deaths (resolvePostCombat, applyVolley shipDestroyed effects) and owns selfDestruct(); it should route every combat/seeking/mine kill through the explosion procedure automatically — no player input is involved.

### 🟠 MED · D9.22 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:800` · _critic_
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**End-of-turn shield repair erases boxes knocked down THIS turn, but D9.22 forbids repairing the current turn's hits.**

D9.22 (PRIOR DAMAGE): damage-control energy 'repairs boxes destroyed in prior turns' and is 'NOT used to reduce the effects of hits made during the turn.' resolveRepairStage() repairs sh.down at end of turn (line 802) with no record of when the box was knocked down, so a shield box destroyed in the same turn is erased immediately. Failure scenario: on turn 3 a Fed CA's #1 shield loses 3 boxes to photon fire; the player allocated 4 DC to shield #1 that same turn; at end of turn 3 the code erases 2 of those turn-3 boxes, when per D9.22 nothing on shield #1 may be repaired until turn 4 (only prior-turn damage qualifies). Needs a per-box damaged-on-turn stamp and a guard that repair only touches boxes damaged before the current turn.


## Energy Allocation & Power (B3/C2/D3/H)

### 🟠 MED · C2.11 / C2.112 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:128`
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**validateEaf never gates movement energy to the warp+impulse sources, so movement can be funded from APR/batteries and can exceed the 30-from-warp cap.**

C2.11 requires energy allocated to movement to come ONLY from warp or impulse engines (never APR or batteries), and C2.112 caps warp-derived movement at 30 points (max practical speed 31 with the single legal impulse point, C2.111). validateEaf (energy-model.js:128-135) sums all sinks against one fungible pool (power.total + batteryCharge) and applies no partition tying `movement` to warp+impulse. The EA-console SPEED stepper (battle.html:1251) uses `sinkMax(p,'movement')` = 31 and the click handler caps at that same value (battle.html:1532), NOT the warp-aware `moveBudget`. Failure scenario: a Fed CA allocates all warp output to weapons/reinforcement and then steps SPEED up to 30-31 in the console; APR/battery power silently funds the movement. validateEaf reports 'balanced', but the ship has moved faster than its warp engines (and C2.11) allow. The warp-aware 'Set speed' modal enforces this correctly, so the two input paths disagree.

- **Automation:** The engine should enforce movement<=warp+impulseMove in validateEaf itself rather than relying on one of two UI paths.

### 🟠 MED · D6.310 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:72`
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**ECM and ECCM are each capped at 6 independently with no combined cap, letting a ship allocate 12 EW points when the rule caps ECM+ECCM combined at the sensor rating.**

D6.310 states the total energy allocated for ECM AND ECCM COMBINED cannot exceed the highest unchecked number on the sensor track (usually 6). sinkMax returns a flat 6 for 'ecm' and again for 'eccm' (energy-model.js:72), and validateEaf performs no combined check. Failure scenario: a Fed CA sets ECM=6 and ECCM=6 in the console, spending 12 energy on EW; validateEaf accepts it though the legal combined maximum is 6. Two sub-defects: (a) no combined cap; (b) the 6 is hard-coded instead of the ship's current highest-unchecked sensor number, so a sensor-damaged ship still gets 6.

- **Automation:** validateEaf should reject (col.ecm+col.eccm) > current sensor rating, and sinkMax should source the ceiling from the sensor track.

### 🟠 MED · D3.343 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:84`
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**A down (0-strength) shield can still receive specific reinforcement, which D3.343 forbids.**

D3.343: a shield that is down or has been dropped cannot be reinforced (only general reinforcement blocks fire from that facing). specReinfMax = p.shields[shieldN-1] (the printed box value), which ignores the shield's CURRENT strength; a shield at 0 still reports its full box value as the specific-reinforcement max, so the console allows allocating specific reinforcement to it. The reactive modal computes a `down` flag (battle.html:1101) but only shows a text note and still lets specific reinforcement through (battle.html:1112,1119). Failure scenario: shield 1 is knocked to 0; the player still pours specific reinforcement into shield 1, gaining protection the rule denies.

- **Automation:** specReinfMax should return 0 when the current shield strength is 0, and the reactive modal should disable the specific stepper for a down shield.

### 🟠 MED · H7.41 / H7.42 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:74` · _critic_
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Reserve warp power is capped in quantity but not gated to warp-engine source, letting warp output be double-committed to movement and reserve simultaneously.**

H7.41/H7.42 require reserve warp power to actually come from warp engines (or warp reactors); APR/impulse/battery power cannot be held as reserve warp. sinkMax caps reserveWarp at p.warp (energy-model.js:74) but validateEaf funds the whole column, reserveWarp included, from total power + batteries (energy-model.js:128-135) with no accounting that warp is a shared source. Failure scenario: warp=8, impulse=2, apr=4, batteries=4. Allocate movement=8 (all warp) and reserveWarp=8; used=lifeSupport+fc+8+8 is covered by total+batteries so it validates and locks. The ship now holds 8 points of 'reserve warp' even though its 8 warp boxes are fully committed to movement — the reserve is really coming from APR/impulse/batteries, which H7.42 forbids.

- **Automation:** No joint warp-source ledger; each warp-fed sink is bounded independently, so warp can be spent twice.

### 🟠 MED · D3.343 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:1112` · _critic_
_missing-gate · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**The reactive reserve-reinforcement modal lets a defender put SPECIFIC reinforcement onto a down (0-strength) shield.**

D3.343 forbids specific reinforcement of a shield that is down; only general reinforcement blocks fire from that facing. promptReserveReinforce computes `down = (shieldsNow(t)[sh-1]||0) <= 0` (battle.html:1101) but never uses it to disable the specific-reinforce stepper: the 'Reinforce shield N' control renders unconditionally (battle.html:1112) and the handler only checks pool affordability (battle.html:1119), so on confirm it adds to t.reinfLeft.spec[sh] (battle.html:1128) for a down shield. This is the reactive reserve code path (distinct from the energy-allocation path); the `down` flag is currently only cosmetic (it tweaks the general-reinforce label).

- **UI:** Defender can illegally reinforce a collapsed shield facing with specific reinforcement in response to incoming fire.

### 🟠 MED · D14.12 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/battle.html:832` · _critic_
_missing-ui · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**EDR auto-repairs arbitrary destroyed boxes rather than the systems the player must specify when allocating lab energy.**

D14.12 requires the player to specify (secretly, when energy is allocated) which destroyed systems each powered lab will attempt to repair, and D14.13 has the target declared before each die roll. The EDR control only lets the player choose a point count (stackCtl 'edr', battle.html:1274/1294); at resolution the engine repairs dead.shift() — i.e., whatever destroyed box happens to be first in status order (battle.html:832) — giving the player no say over which system (e.g. a phaser vs. a warp box) is repaired. Rating cap (D14.24) and one-roll-per-lab (D14.13) are otherwise handled correctly.

- **UI:** No control to designate EDR repair targets; the engine picks for the player.
- **Automation:** Target choice that the rules assign to the player is silently automated to an arbitrary order.

### 🟡 LOW · D3.342 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:84`
_wrong-constant · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Specific reinforcement is capped at the shield's printed box value, but D3.342 places no such cap.**

D3.342: specific reinforcement adds 'extra' boxes equal to the amount of energy applied, for the duration of the turn, limited only by available power. specReinfMax = p.shields[shieldN-1] artificially caps allocation at the shield's box value. Failure scenario: a player with surplus power wants to over-reinforce a facing beyond its printed strength (a legal defensive move) and the console's +stepper refuses past the box count. The audit already lists this (rules-audit.md:137) and it remains open in the current code.

- **Automation:** The specific-reinforcement ceiling should be available power, not the shield box value.

### 🟡 LOW · C2.411 / C2.112 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:163`
_wrong-constant · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**foldEaf caps folded speed at 30 though maximum practical speed is 31 (30 warp + 1 impulse).**

C2.411/C2.112 allow speed 31 (the 31st hex from one impulse point). foldEaf computes speed = Math.min(30, floor(movement/moveCost)), discarding a legal 31st hex. sinkMax('movement') and validateEaf already allow 31, and the plotted-speed path supports 31, so the min(30,...) only bites the no-plot fallback that seeds s.speedPlot.base (battle.html:1579) — but there it silently drops the ship from a legal 31 to 30.

- **Automation:** Cap should be 31, matching sinkMax and the movement ceiling.

## Weapon Arming (E/FP)

### 🟠 MED · E4.44 / E4.413 · `ssd-pipeline/viewer/energy-model.js:124`
_wrong-constant · votes 3/3 · confidence high_

**Holding a fully-armed OVERLOADED photon is charged 1 energy/turn instead of the required 2.**

E4.44 says an overloaded photon is held at the cost shown in the E4.413 chart; for a full (8-point) overload that hold cost is 2/turn (the chart's Standard column is 2 across every overload level).
